The Viksit Bharat–Guarantee for Rozgar and Ajeevika Mission (Gramin) Act, 2025 (VB-GRAM-G) marks a major shift in India’s rural welfare architecture. Enacted in December 2025 and implemented from July 1, 2026, it replaces MGNREGA (2005), reorienting rural employment from a welfare safety net to a productivity-driven development tool aligned with Viksit Bharat @ 2047.

## Key Provisions

* **Employment Guarantee:** Increased from 100 to 125 days per rural household.
* **Funding Model:** Shifts from demand-driven to “normative allocation” with pre-fixed state-wise caps; excess expenditure to be borne by states.
* **Cost Sharing:** 60:40 (Centre:States); 90:10 for NE and Himalayan states.
* **Agricultural Pause:** Provision for up to a 60-day “agricultural pause” during peak farming seasons.

## Asset Creation and Planning

* Focus on durable, productivity-enhancing assets under “Rozgar Bhi, Samman Bhi”.
* Four priority areas: water security, rural infrastructure, livelihood infrastructure, and climate resilience.
* Decentralised planning via Viksit Gram Panchayat Plans (VGPPs), integrated with PM Gati Shakti and VB-NRIS.

## Governance and Transparency

* Mandatory biometric attendance, NMMS-based monitoring, digital muster rolls, and geotagging.
* DBT-based wage payments within 15 days.
* Strengthened transparency through Janata Boards and social audits.

## Labour Rights

* Guaranteed worksite facilities: water, shade, first aid.
* Childcare provision where ≥5 children are present.
* Compensation framework: delay penalty (0.05% per day), unemployment allowance, and accident coverage including PMSBY benefits.

## Critical Analysis

### Positives
* Transforms welfare into asset-led rural development.
* Aligns employment generation with infrastructure and climate goals.
* Reduces leakages through tech-enabled governance.
* Addresses farm labour shortages via the agricultural pause.

### Concerns
* Normative allocation weakens the demand-driven safety net, risking exclusion during distress.
* Greater fiscal burden on states may create federal tensions and uneven implementation.

## Conclusion

VB-GRAM-G represents a paradigm shift from entitlement-based employment to productivity-linked rural development. Its effectiveness will hinge on fair allocation norms and states’ fiscal capacity to sustain the expanded guarantee.
